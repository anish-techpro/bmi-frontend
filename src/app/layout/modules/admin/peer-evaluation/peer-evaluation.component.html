The following grade descriptors and notes regarding typical grade distribution are intended to help orient faculty members when assigning grades, and also to ensure consistency of grading throughout the programme.

Grade Descriptor
10 Work clearly, coherently and systematically responds to the task(s), demonstrating a sophisticated command of relevant complex knowledge and skills at a level exceeding what is required, as well as strong ability to formulate and substantiate an independent judgement.
9 Work clearly, coherently and systematically responds to the task(s), demonstrating strong command of relevant complex knowledge and skills as well as an ability to formulate and substantiate an independent judgement.
8 Work coherently responds to the task(s), demonstrating command of relevant knowledge and skills as well as some ability to formulate and substantiate an independent judgement beyond mere recapitulation of material.
7 Work responds to the task(s) coherently and accurately, in most cases, demonstrating basic command of relevant knowledge and skills as well as some independent thinking beyond mere recapitulation of material.
6 Work in most cases responds accurately to the task(s), mainly recapitulating material in a descriptive manner, demonstrating minimum command of relevant knowledge and skills.
5 Work demonstrates comprehension of the task(s), recapitulating material in a merely descriptive manner and not entirely accurately, presenting unsubstantiated opinion as evaluation and/or failing to consider important relevant aspects.
4 Work shows partial comprehension of the task(s) and attempts to recapitulate material, but generally fails to propose a coherent response, ignoring or misrepresenting relevant aspects.
3 Work shows limited comprehension of the task(s) and some attempt to recapitulate material, proposing responses that are to a large degree incomplete and/or irrelevant.
2 There is almost no evidence of an attempt to tackle the task(s) set. There is almost nothing of relevance in the work presented or submitted.
1 There is no evidence of an attempt to tackle the task(s) set. There is nothing of relevance in the work presented or submitted.
